Salmon

Grilled Salmon Directions 1 Preheat grill to 400 to 450°. 2 Brush the salmon evenly with 2 tablespoon olive oil; sprinkle with salt and pepper. Stir together the vinegar, remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, mustard, and paprika. Set aside. 3 When the grill is hot enough, oil the grates. Place salmon on the grill grates skin side down; brush with the vinegar mixture. Cover, and grill until the salmon releases from the grill with a gentle nudge, about 5 minutes. Flip the salmon, and grill until the salmon is cooked through to your desired doneness, 2 to 3 minutes more. 4 Garnish with parsley and season with more salt, if you like.

Bacon Biscuit How to grill bacon Heat the grill to its highest setting. Line a baking sheet with foil and add the bacon. Grill for 2-4 mins on each side, depending on how crispy you like it.

Shrimp

Grilled Shrimp For 1 pound shrimp, in a 3-quart saucepan, bring 4 cups water and 1 teaspoon salt to boiling. Add shrimp. Simmer, uncovered, 1 to 3 minutes or until shrimp turn opaque, stirring occasionally. Drain and rinse shrimp in a colander under cold running water.

Prawns

Fried Prawns Coat the prawns completely in flour before dipping into the egg. Dip quickly in the egg so that the flour doesn’t dissolve in the egg. Use panko breadcrumbs that make the prawns extra crispy when fried. Chili powder added to the flour and bread crumbs gives additional flavor and attractive golden color to the fried prawns. Optionally, you can add paprika or “Kashmiri chili powder” if you want even more color but with less heat. Adding flavor to the outer layer is the best way to making the most out of breaded and fried recipes, like the Italian potato croquettes.
